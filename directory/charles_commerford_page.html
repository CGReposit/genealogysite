Historical Notes

Charles C Commerford lived through one of the most transformative periods in American history, from before the Civil War through World War I. He witnessed the nation's industrial revolution and tremendous social change.

Political Career: After achieving some political successes in his community, Charles was appointed postmaster by President Grover Cleveland in 1886. This appointment came during Cleveland's first term as president (1885-1889), when political patronage was a common practice for rewarding party loyalists.

The postmaster position was a prestigious and important role in 19th-century America, as the post office was the primary means of communication and connection between communities.
