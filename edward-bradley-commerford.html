Historical Notes

Edward Bradley Commerford, affectionately known as "Pali," was born on 17 July 1918 in Jersey City, Hudson County, New Jersey. At the time of his birth, his father John Rowe Commerford was 34 years old and his mother Ellen Mae "Ella" Youmans was 26.

Early Life and Residence: Edward grew up in the greater New York/New Jersey area during the interwar period. He lived in Westwood, Bergen County, New Jersey for approximately 30 years, where he raised his family and built his life.

Military Service: In 1942, during World War II, Edward registered for military service, contributing to his country during a pivotal moment in history. He was 24 years old at the time of his registration.

Family Life: Edward married Jeannette Angela Malcuit, whose family had immigrated to the United States from France and Italy. Together they had at least one son, Brad, who would continue the Commerford family line.

Later Years: In his later years, Edward relocated to Washington State, where he lived in 1999. He passed away on 5 June 1999 at the age of 80 and was buried in Paramus, Bergen County, New Jersey, returning to rest in the state where he had spent most of his life.

Family Legacy: As part of the Commerford family lineage that traces back to John Commerford (1800-1878), Edward represented the continuation of a family with deep roots in the New York/New Jersey area.
