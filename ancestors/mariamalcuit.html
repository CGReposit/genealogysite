Historical Notes

Maria Malcuit worked as a cook according to Ellis Island immigration documentation.

She arrived in the United States on January 27th, 1906, traveling with her two young children. Records indicate she was en route to meet her husband, who had arrived earlier and was already in New York.

Immigration documents note that she carried approximately $12 upon arrival—although the exact figure is difficult to interpret due to handwriting.
