Historical Notes

Summary: Fanny (Francis) O’Neill was born on 20 December 1864 in Bergen County, New Jersey, during the American Civil War. She was the daughter of Dennis O’Neill and Mary Jane Ring of Irish descent.

She married George E. Youmans in 1889, and they had at least three children, including Ellen Mae “Ella” Youmans (later Commerford). After George’s death in 1899, she later married Perry Edward Bradley, a widower with three sons from a prior marriage.

She lived in Jersey City and Westwood, Bergen County, for much of her life and is remembered as a resilient woman who bridged two families through hardship and remarriage.
