Historical Notes

John Rowe Commerford was born on 29 May 1884 in New Jersey to Charles C Commerford, who was 50 years old at the time, and Elizabeth Hamilton, who was approximately 25. He was the son of a notable father—Charles was a former professional baseball player for the New York Gothams and later became postmaster, appointed by President Grover Cleveland in 1886.

First Marriage and Ohio Years: On 3 October 1907, John married Grace Truman Duvall in Muskingum County, Ohio. Grace was from Ohio, and John relocated to the state following their marriage. Tragically, Grace passed away in 1911 at the age of 29, leaving John a widower after just four years of marriage.

Second Marriage and Family Life: John later married Ellen Mae "Ella" Youmans (born 17 May 1892), and together they built a large family. When their son Edward Bradley was born on 17 July 1918 in Jersey City, Hudson County, New Jersey, John was 34 years old and Ellen was 26. The couple had five children together: Carol (1914–1989), Ruth (b. 1914), John "Jack" Jr. (1917–2002), Edward "Pali" (1918–1999), and Robert "Bob" (1926–2019).

Military Service: John served his country during World War I from 1917 to 1918, contributing to the war effort during a pivotal time in American history.

Business Career: By the time of the 1920 Census, John had established himself as a successful businessman. He listed his occupation as President of his own Lithography Company, demonstrating entrepreneurial achievement and leadership in the printing industry.

Residence: The Commerford family lived at 5 Park Avenue in Westwood, Bergen County, New Jersey—a testament to John's professional success and social standing in the community.

John Rowe Commerford passed away on 29 January 1968 at the age of 83, having lived through two world wars, the Great Depression, and remarkable social change. He was laid to rest at Pascack Cemetery in Park Ridge, Bergen County, New Jersey, alongside other members of the Commerford and Youmans families.
